About Theyyam

Theyyam is a spectacular ritual art form that flourishes in the Northern districts of Kerala, especially in Kannur and Kasargod. Rooted deeply in folk traditions, Theyyam is more than just a performance - it's a living religious tradition that blends dance, music, mime, and spiritual worship. With its vivid costumes, intense makeup, and powerful choreography, Theyyam stands as a profound expression of devotion, folklore, and community identity.
The word Theyyam is derived from the Sanskrit word Daivam, meaning God. Performers are considered incarnations of deities, ancestral spirits, or heroic figures from local myths. When a performer dons the elaborate costume and face paint, they are believed to be temporarily possessed by the divine spirit they represent, and devotees approach them to seek blessings, answers, and healing.

Performance and Rituals

Theyyam is performed in sacred groves(kaavus), village temples, or ancestral homes. The season typically begins in October and continues till May. Each performancefollows rituals, including chanting, drumming (mainly chenda and elathalam), and sometimes fire-walking or sword-wielding dances. The colorful headgear, intricate face paiinting, and rhythmic movements create a mesmerixing experience for spectators.

Theyyam has a long history. "There can be no doubt", say Bridget and Raymond Alchin, "that a very large part of this modern folk religion is extremely ancient and contains traits which originated during the earliest periods of Neolithic, Chalcolithic settlement and expression.
Theyyam may have its origins from ancestor worship. It has been observed that a vast majority of the Theyyam deities have their origin from Thiyya and Nair castes. The ancestor worship ritual was later evolved into the elaborate dance ritual that is seen nowadays. Incorporating many other local beliefs.
